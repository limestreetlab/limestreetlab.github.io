  • Programming the World Wide Web
  • Robert Sebesta
  • First-year college textbook style introducing the web. Chapter 1 introduces the server-client model, URLs, and HTTP, followed by individual chapters on client-side and server-side technologies, ranging from HTML/CSS and JS to PHP and Ruby, one chapter each. Good thing is that the chapters are self-contained so can easily skip some, but the contents are very basic and merely enough to get a shallow sense. Written in 2018, I wonder why it includes obsolete topics (by many years) like Java Servlets, JavaBeans, Flash, XML over JSON, Dojo toolkit over JQuery. The book is evenly split between useless stuff like HTML/CSS, obsolete topics like JSP, and relevant yet shallow discussions like PHP/Ruby/JDBC.
