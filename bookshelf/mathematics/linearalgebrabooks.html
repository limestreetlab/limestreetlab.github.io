  • No Bullshit Guide to Linear Algebra
  • Ivan Savov
  • Effective book written by an experienced tutor rather than a university professor. Theoretically sound contents delivered using a personal style, making abstract concepts like vector spaces more digestable. Instead of giving definitions and proofs like many texts, author separates the big picture from detailed calculations and focuses on intuition or geometric motivation. Colour is added to each topic by either explaining why something is defined that way, what it represents, or what it is for; he then introduces the calculations, with short examples and sometimes even using SymPy. Instead of theories, author encourages visualization and understanding of basic ideas.

    Author explains u ⋅ v as a measure of similarity in terms of u and v directions (one normalized as a unit vector), |A| as volume of a shape whose edges are row vectors of A with value of |A| as volume scaling factor and sign of |A| based on angles of the edges so |A|=0 implies parallel edges, eigenvectors as special direction-preserving vectors used in PageRank.

    Unfortunately, there are some careless and confusing edit typos. The lack of proofs for key concepts can also hinder thorough understanding. To be used as a guide, not main text.

  • Linear Algebra and its Applications
  • David Lay
  • Covers introductory topics from vector spaces and linear maps to diagonalization and symmetric/orthogonal matrices in a concise, intuitive, and easy-to-understand manner. Concepts and definitions are introduced and discussed clearly step-by-step accompanied by short examples and sometimes longer applications such as linear regression parameters as orthogonal projections and quadratic forms in constrained optimization.

    It contains plenty of exercises including end-of-section short problems, and end-of-section and end-of-chapter exercises (solutions in manual). It has all important pedagocical elements, so if I have to pick just one book, this is it; if I can pick another one, that will be Martin Anthony as a supplement.
