During a crime investigation, the inspector thinks the current suspect is $60\%$ probable of being guilty.
A new piece of evidence from the crime scene emerges showing that the criminal has a certain hair texture.
If $20\%$ of the population has that hair texture, how confident should the inspector be if the current suspect has the texture.
Let $G, E$ denote guilty and the hair texture, then $$ P(G) = 0.6, P(G^c) = 0.4, P(E|G) = 1, P(E|G^c) = 0.2 $$ Plugging in, \begin{align} P(G|E) &= \frac{ P(E|G)P(G) }{ P(E|G)P(G) + P(E|G^c)P(G^c) } \\ &= \frac{ (1)(0.6) }{ (1)(0.6) + (0.2)(0.4) } \\ &= \frac{0.6}{0.68} \approx 0.88 \end{align}
