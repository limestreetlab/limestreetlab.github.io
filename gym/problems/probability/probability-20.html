Seven pathetic human beings carrying guns call themselves hunters are waiting for birds to fly by.
When birds fly over, they independently and randomly aim at one of the birds.
Let each cowardly hunter's hit probability be $p$.
A group of $10$ birds are to fly by, what's the expected number of them escaping safely (unhit).
Let $X = [0, 10]$ be the number of birds unhit.
Let $X_i$ be an indicator variable to indicate if $i^{th}$ bird escapes unhit, $$ X_i = \begin{cases} 1 \quad \text{if $i^{th}$ bird unhit} \\ 0 \quad \text{if $i^{th}$ bird hit} \end{cases} $$ Hence, the number of the $10$ birds escaping unhit is $$ X = X_1 + X_2 + \ldots + X_9 + X_{10} $$ Now, for each hunter, a bird is chosen with probability $\frac{1}{10}$ and hit at $p$.
So for each hunter, $i^{th}$ bird will be aimed at and hit with probability $\frac{p}{10}$ and unhit with probability $\left(1 - \frac{p}{10} \right)$.
Hence, $i^{th}$ bird unhit by 7 hunters is, $$ P(X_i = 1) = \left( 1-\frac{p}{10} \right)^{7} $$ and so, $$ E(X) = E(X_1) + \ldots + E(X_{10}) = 10 \cdot \left( 1-\frac{p}{10} \right)^{7} $$
