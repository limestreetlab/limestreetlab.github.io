An urn contains 2 type-A coins and 1 type-B coin.
The probability of heads for type-A and type-B coins are $\frac{1}{4} and \frac{3}{4}$, respectively.
A coin randomly picked and flipped shows a head.
What's the probability that the coin is type-A?
Letting $A, B, H$ denote type-A coin, type-B coin, and head, then, $$ P(A) = \frac{2}{3}, P(B) = \frac{1}{3}, P(H|A) = \frac{1}{4}, P(H|B) = \frac{3}{4} $$ Hence, \begin{align} P(A|H) & = \frac{P(H|A)P(A)}{P(H|A)P(A) + P(H|B)P(B)} \\ &= \frac{ \frac{1}{4}\frac{2}{3} } { \frac{1}{4}\frac{2}{3} + \frac{3}{4}\frac{1}{3} } \\ &= \frac{ \frac{2}{12} } { \frac{5}{12} } = \frac{2}{5} \end{align}
