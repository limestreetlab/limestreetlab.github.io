Two fair dice are rolled sequentially. What's the probability that the second die lands on a higher value than that of the first?
For the 2nd die to be higher than the 1st,

If 1st die is $1$, then 2nd must be $ \{ 2,3,4,5,6 \} $.
If 1st die is $2$, then 2nd must be $ \{ 3,4,5,6 \} $.
$ldots$
If 1st die is $5$, then 2nd must be $ \{ 6 \} $.
If 1st die is $6$, then 2nd cannot have a higher value. Hence, the desired probability is \begin{align} & \frac{1}{6}\frac{5}{6} + \frac{1}{6}\frac{4}{6} +\frac{1}{6}\frac{3}{6} +\frac{1}{6}\frac{2}{6} +\frac{1}{6}\frac{1}{6} + 0 \\ & \approx 0.42 \end{align}
