Five people, say A, B, C, D, E, are randomly arranged in linear order.
What's the probability that
  1. there is one person between A and B.
  2. there are two persons between A and B.
  3. there are three persons between A and B.
a. First, just consider the case where A presedes B. A few example arrangements are $$ \{A, C, B, E, D \} \quad \{C, E, A, D, B \} \quad \{D, A, C, B, E \} $$ The point is to treat $\{A X B \}$ as one element where $X$ is a wildcard. Then
  • there are $3!$ arrangements for the $3$ elements
  • there are $3$ possible arrangements for the wildcard
  • A and B can swap positions for $\{A X B \}$ or $\{B X A \}$
Hence, $$ \left( \frac{3! \cdot 3 \cdot 2}{5!} \right) = 0.3 $$ b.
  • there are $2!$ arrangements for the $2$ elements
  • there are $3 \cdot 2$ possible arrangements for the wildcards
  • A and B can swap positions for $\{A X X B \}$ or $\{B X X A \}$
Hence, $$ \left( \frac{2! \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 2}{5!} \right) = 0.2 $$ c.
  • there is $1!$ arrangement for the $1$ element
  • there are $3!$ possible arrangements for the wildcards
  • A and B can swap positions for $\{A X X X B \}$ or $\{B X X X A \}$
Hence, $$ \left( \frac{1! \cdot 3! \cdot 2}{5!} \right) = 0.1 $$
