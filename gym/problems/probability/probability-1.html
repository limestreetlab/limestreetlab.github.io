A crime occurs at a point $X$ that is uniformly distributed on a street of length $K$.
A police officier is at point $Y$ that is also uniformly distributed on the street.
If $X$ and $Y$ are independent, what is the expected distance between the crime spot and police officier.
The expected distance is $E(|X - Y |)$.
Given independence, the joint pdf is, $$f_{X,Y}(x,y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y) = \frac{1}{K} \frac{1}{K} = \frac{1}{K^2}, \qquad \text{0 $<$ x, y $<$ K} $$ Hence, $$ E(|E-Y|) = \int_0^K \int_0^K \left(|x - y| \frac{1}{K^2}\right) dx dy $$ Now, \begin{align} \int_0^K |x-y|dx &= \int_0^y(y-x)dx + \int_y^K(x-y)dx \qquad \text{as $|x - y| = (y-x)$ when $x < y$} \\ &= (yx - \frac{x^K}{2})\bigg|_0^y + (\frac{x^2}{2} - yx)\bigg|_y^K \\ &= y^2 -\frac{y^2}{2} + \frac{K^2}{2} - yK - \frac{y^2}{2} + y^2 \\ &= 2y^2 + \frac{K^2}{2} - y^2 - yK \\ &= \frac{K^2}{2} + y^2 - yK \end{align} Therefore, \begin{align} E(|E - Y|) &= \frac{1}{K^2}\int_0^K \left(\frac{K^2}{2} + y^2 - yK \right ) dy \\ &= \frac{1}{K^2}\left(\frac{yK^2}{2} + \frac{y^3}{3} - \frac{y^2K}{2} \right)\Bigg|_0^K \\ &= \frac{1}{K^2} \frac{K^3}{3} \\ &= \frac{K}{3} \end{align}
