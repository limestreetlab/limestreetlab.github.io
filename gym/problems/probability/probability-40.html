Sally and Tony alternate rolling a pair of dice, stopping when either Sally rolls a $9$ sum or Tony a $6$ sum.
Suppose Sally rolls first, what's the probability that
  1. The game stops at Sally?
  2. The game stops at Tony?
a.
We have \begin{align} P(9_{sum}) & = P(\{3,6\},\{4,5\},\{5,4\},\{6,3\}) = \frac{4}{36} \qquad && P(9_{sum}^c) = \frac{32}{36} \\ P(6_{sum}) & = P(\{1,5\},\{2,4\},\{3,3\},\{4,2\}, \{5,1\}) = \frac{5}{36} \qquad && P(6_{sum}^c) = \frac{31}{36} \end{align}
The cases that Sally rolls her $9$ sum first are \begin{align} &P(9_{sum}) \\ &P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}) \\ &P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}) \\ &P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}^c) \cdot P(6_{sum}^c) \cdot P(9_{sum}) \\ &\ldots \end{align} Using the geometric series formula $\text{sum} = \frac{a(1-r^{n+1})}{1-r}$ where $n = \infty$ here, we get $$ P(\text{Sally}) = \frac{4/36(1-((32/36)(31/36))^\infty)}{1-(32/36)(31/36)} \approx 0.47 $$ b.
$$ P(\text{Tony}) = \frac{5/36(1-((32/36)(31/36))^\infty)}{1-(32/36)(31/36)} \approx 0.59 $$
