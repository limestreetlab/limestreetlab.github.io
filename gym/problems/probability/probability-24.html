We have a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards. We turn up cards from the deck one at a time until an ace appears.
Once we stop, is the next card in the deck —that is, the card following the first ace— more likely to be the ace of spade or the king of heart?
We first consider how many of the $52!$ possible orderings of cards have the ace of spade immediately after the first ace.
For each of the $51!$ orderings of the other cards, there is only $1$ slot the ace of spade can be placed such that it follows the first ace.
For example, if the ordering of the $51$ other cards is $$ 4c, 8h, Kd, 4d, 5s, Ac, 8s, \dots, Jh, 10d $$ then the only slot for ace of spade to result in its following the first ace is $$ 4c, 8h, Kd, 4d, 5s, Ac, As, 8s, \dots, Jh, 10d $$ Hence, there are $51!$ orderings of the ace of spade following the first ace, so $$ P(\text{ace of spade following the first ace}) = \frac{51!}{52!} = \frac{1}{52} $$
By exactly the same argument, it follows that the probability that the king of heart (or any other specific card) follows the first ace is also $\frac{1}{52}$.
That is to say, each of the $52$ cards following the first ace is equally likely.
