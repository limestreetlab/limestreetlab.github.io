Spencer has a long position in a stock currently trading at \$50.
He will take profit at \$65 or stop loss at \$35, a $\pm \$15$ P&L window.
Assume price movements are discrete at \$1-step and independent, with probabilities 0.55 up and 0.45 down.
What's the probability that this stock will end up a winner?
Recall the gambler's ruin problem and its formula $\frac{1-(q/p)^i}{1-(q/p)^N}$ where $i$ is the initial stake, $N$ the total stake to win, $p$ the probability of a win, and $q$ the probability of a loss.

Applying in this question, $ \frac{q}{p} = \frac{0.45}{0.55} = \frac{9}{11} $, $i = 15$, $N = 30$ , $$ P(\text{take profit}) = \frac{1 - (9/11)^{15}}{1 - (9/11)^{30}} \approx 0.95 $$
